# How to Prepare Your Nonclinical Overview Template

## Important: Template Preparation Required

The system now uses **placeholder-based filling** to preserve your entire template structure including:
- ✅ Table of Contents
- ✅ List of Tables/Figures
- ✅ List of Abbreviations
- ✅ All sections and formatting
- ✅ Page numbers and headers
- ✅ Your custom text and instructions

## Step 1: Add Placeholders to Your Template

## Step 3: Save Your Template

1. Add all placeholders where you want content
2. Keep all your existing text, formatting, tables, headers
3. Save as `.docx` file
4. Upload to the system

## Step 4: Use the Template

1. Search for articles
2. Add to cart
3. Click "Template Doc"
4. Upload your prepared template
5. Generate filled document

## Tips

✅ **DO:**
- Keep all your template structure intact
- Add placeholders where you want article content
- Keep instructional text (will remain in final document)
- Use standard placeholder names from the list above
- Test with a simple article first

❌ **DON'T:**
- Remove sections or formatting
- Delete Table of Contents or Lists
- Change placeholder syntax (must be `{placeholder_name}`)
- Expect full article text (only abstracts available)

## What Gets Filled

The system extracts content from **article abstracts** using intelligent keyword matching:

- **Pharmacology** → sentences mentioning mechanism, receptors, activity
- **Pharmacokinetics** → sentences about ADME, half-life, bioavailability
- **Toxicology** → sentences about safety, adverse effects, toxicity

If no relevant content is found, placeholder shows: "No specific information available in the article abstract."
